Epidemiological Trends of Viral Hemorrhagic Fevers in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Longitudinal Analysis of Healthcare Infrastructure Impact

Okonjo A. Bello1,2*, Chima E. Okafor1, Fatima S. Abubakar3, Zainab T. Yusuf4

DOI

https://doi.org/10.5555/nimasrepub.2024.10234

Published

October 24, 2024

Abstract

This study examines the evolving landscape of Viral Hemorrhagic Fevers (VHFs) across West and Central Africa from 2014 to 2024. By synthesizing epidemiological data from fourteen regional health ministries, we analyze the correlation between healthcare facility density and case-fatality rates. Our findings indicate a statistically significant reduction in latency periods for outbreak reporting in regions with integrated genomic surveillance networks. The paper concludes that localized laboratory capacity is the primary determinant of containment success, surpassing traditional resource-based predictors.
Keywords: Genomics Epidemiology Lassa Fever Public Health

Introduction

Viral hemorrhagic fevers represent a persistent challenge to global health security. In the Nigerian context, annual outbreaks of Lassa fever necessitate a robust and reactive diagnostic framework. Despite significant advancements in mobile healthcare delivery, the structural disparities between urban centers and rural hinterlands remain a critical variable in viral propagation dynamics.
